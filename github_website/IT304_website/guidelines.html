IT Ethics Guidelines

Ethical guidelines for IT computer professionals to follow

Contribute to society and the well-being of humankind

Computing professionals should be using their skills to benefit society, which includes promoting human rights, protecting autonomy, and minimizing negative consequences. Considerations of whether their work will be used responsibly, be accessible, and promote sustainability.

Avoid harm

Computing professionals work should avoid harming which includes physical, mental, unjustified damages, and much more. Following generally accepted best practices will help minimize indirect or unintentional harm to others. Computing Professionals also have an obligation to note and report risks in systems that potentially can lead to harm.When reporting system risks, a careful assessment should be made before reporting.

Honesty and Trust

Computing Professionals should always be transparent and disclose all of their systems abilities, limits, and potential issues. Never should a Computing Professinal ever falsify data, make misleading claims, or bribe others for their system, as these are violations of the Code of Ethics. A professionals should also be open about their qualifications and their limitations in their ability to do a task.
